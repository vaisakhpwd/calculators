    About

    A cantilever type RCC (Reinforced Concrete) retaining wall is a type of structure used to retain soil and other materials. It derives its name from the structural principle of a cantilever beam, where a horizontal structure (base slab) extends into the retained soil and supports a vertical stem that resists the lateral pressure exerted by the soil.


    Parts of Cantilever Retaining Wall

    • Stem: The vertical part of the wall that retains the soil. It is designed to withstand bending moments and shear forces caused by the lateral pressure of the soil.
    • Base Slab: The horizontal part of the wall that extends outward into the retained soil. It provides stability by counteracting the overturning moment generated by the soil pressure against the stem.
    • Toe: The lower part of the base slab that extends beyond the face of the wall into the retained soil. It helps in preventing sliding of the wall.
    • Heel: The part of the base slab that is inside the retained soil, located behind the stem. It resists the overturning moment exerted by the soil pressure.
    • Key: It is the small intrusive structure of a retaining wall, sometimes build at bottom of the base in order to increase the passive lateral force and resisting sliding and shear.
